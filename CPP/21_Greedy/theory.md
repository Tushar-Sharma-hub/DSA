# Greedy Theory

- Strategy: make the locally optimal choice at each step and hope it leads to a correct global solution.
- Common pattern: sort candidates and then iterate, selecting the best available option as you go.
- Examples: choose the earliest finishing interval, take the largest or smallest available resource, or fill capacity greedily.
- Validation: greedy works when the problem has the greedy choice property and optimal substructure.
- Typical use: interval scheduling, coin change with canonical systems, and resource allocation problems.
